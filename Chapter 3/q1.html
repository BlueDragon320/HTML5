            Dosa is a popular South Indian dish, loved for its simplicity, versatility, and rich flavors. It is a type of fermented crepe made from a batter of rice and urad dal (black gram lentils). The fermentation process gives the dosa its characteristic tangy taste, while also making it a nutritious meal, rich in proteins, carbohydrates, and essential vitamins.

Traditionally, dosa is served with accompaniments like sambar (a spiced lentil soup) and chutneys made from coconut, tomatoes, or mint. It can be enjoyed plain or stuffed with fillings, the most famous being the masala dosa, which contains a spicy potato mixture.

Dosas have transcended their regional origins, becoming a beloved dish across India and globally. Its versatility allows for various modern twists, with fillings ranging from paneer and cheese to international flavors like Mexican or Italian-inspired stuffings.
